Weather is the daily, temporary state of the atmosphere, encompassing temperature, humidity, precipitation, cloudiness, visibility, and wind. 
It is constantly changing, driven by air pressure differences, and shapes Earth's surface through erosion. 
Weather is distinct from climate, which represents long-term averages.
Key components of weather include:
Temperature: How hot or cold the air is, measured by thermometers.
Humidity: The amount of water vapor in the air.
Wind: Movement of air caused by pressure differences, measured by speed and direction.
Precipitation: Water, in liquid or solid form, that falls from the atmosphere.
Atmospheric Pressure: The weight of the air, often indicating clear skies (high pressure) or storms (low pressure).
Clouds: Formed by rising and cooling air, affecting solar energy absorption.
